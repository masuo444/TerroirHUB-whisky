WHISKY GUIDE — CHAPTER 01

Types of
Japanese Whisky

Japanese whisky falls into five main categories. Understanding the differences in ingredients, distillation, and blending will help you find the perfect bottle for your palate.

5 TYPES

The Five Types of Japanese Whisky

From distillery-character Single Malts to one-of-a-kind Single Casks and the new wave of Craft distilleries.

TYPE 01
Single Malt

Made exclusively from 100% malted barley and distilled at a single distillery using pot stills (batch distillation). The distillery's water source, climate, still shape, and cask selection are all directly reflected in the flavor, making single malt the type that most clearly expresses a distillery's identity.

Yamazaki is known for its complexity from sherry and Mizunara casks. Hakushu offers a fresh, forest-like character. Yoichi stands out with its powerful coal-fired distillation. Recent craft distilleries are also creating their own distinctive single malts.

Notable brands: Yamazaki, Hakushu, Yoichi, Miyagikyo, Ichiro's Malt
TYPE 02
Single Grain

Distilled from grains such as corn or wheat using a column still (continuous distillation). Compared to malt whisky, grain whisky has a lighter, cleaner taste. While traditionally rarely consumed on its own, single grain bottlings like Chita have become increasingly popular in recent years.

In blended whisky, grain serves as the essential foundation, balancing and complementing the malt's character while bringing overall harmony to the blend.

Notable brands: Chita (Suntory), Fuji (Kirin)
TYPE 03
Blended

A masterful combination of malt and grain whiskies. Through the blender's skill and artistry, blended whisky achieves a complexity and balance that no single spirit could deliver alone.

Blended whisky accounts for the majority of Japanese whisky consumption. Hibiki is recognized as one of the world's finest blended whiskies. The highball boom has also been driven primarily by blended whisky.

Notable brands: Hibiki, Kakubin, Black Nikka, From The Barrel
TYPE 04
Single Cask

Bottled from one individual cask, making each release truly unique. Even whiskies from the same distillery and the same process will taste completely different from cask to cask — a genuinely once-in-a-lifetime experience.

Often bottled at cask strength (without dilution), reaching alcohol levels of 50-60%. Venture Whisky's Ichiro's Malt has captivated collectors worldwide with its acclaimed single cask releases.

Notable brands: Ichiro's Malt Card Series
TYPE 05
Craft Whisky

Since the 2010s, small-scale distilleries have been springing up across Japan. A 2016 revision to the liquor tax law made distillery licenses more accessible, further fueling the movement. As of 2024, over 100 distillery projects are operating or under construction nationwide.

Akkeshi (Hokkaido) draws inspiration from Scotland's Islay with its coastal location. Nagahama (Shiga) operates one of Japan's smallest stills. Saburomaru (Toyama) uses the world's first cast-metal pot still. Each distillery takes a unique approach, expanding the diversity of Japanese whisky.

Notable distilleries: Akkeshi, Nagahama, Saburomaru, Shizuoka, Kanosuke
