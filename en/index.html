TYPES

Types of Japanese Whisky

Distinct styles defined by ingredients, distillation, and cask selection

🥃
Single Malt
Made exclusively from malted barley at a single distillery. Yamazaki, Yoichi, and Hakushu are the iconic expressions. Each distillery's character shines through.
🌾
Grain
Distilled from corn or other grains in a continuous still. Light and clean in character. Chita is the benchmark. The unsung hero of blended whiskies.
🍸
Blended
A masterful marriage of malt and grain whiskies. Hibiki and Nikka From The Barrel showcase the art of Japanese blending at its finest.
🪨
Single Cask
Bottled from one individual cask. Each is unique, even within the same brand. Ichiro's Malt is celebrated for its exceptional single cask releases.
🏠
Craft
Japan's craft distillery boom since the 2010s. Akkeshi, Nagahama, Saburomaru and others bring fresh perspectives, using local water and climate.
